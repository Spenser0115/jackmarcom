A Monsters Love

- Two boys in foster care help each other overcome their traumas while being subtly guided by the manifestations of their nightmares.

INT. UNDERPASS TUNNEL
A can of spray paint clinks onto the cement floor as Harley pulls the flashlight from his backpack. The two boys walk down the tunnel, scanning the graffiti like ancient texts, and stop at a semi-clear spot on the wall.
GRAYSON
Here. This is perfect.
Harley shines his light around the tunnel, uneasy with the darkness. He crosses a graffiti monster and jumps. Grayson rattles his can and laughs to himself under his breath.
GRAYSON
Have you figured out what to draw?
Harley turns back to Grayson and shrugs. Behind him, a familiar shadow moves in the dark, and the monster on the walls eyes glow with a faint gold.
GRAYSON
Whatever we put will be here for a long time, I might still have a piece or two somewhere, yuck, too embarrassing to think about.
Harley rests his face against the can and purses his lips.
GRAYSON
I got it!
His exclamation echoes for miles. He sets down his flashlight and steps up to the wall. Harley inches backwards into the dark to watch, and smoke rises around his feet. Grayson swings his arms like a bad dancer, full of passion and with no direction. He sprays streaks across the wall, waltzing with his can and humming a tango.
GRAYSON
Almost there.
He leaps side to side, crossing into other works. Harley echoes a laugh through the tunnel that shocks Delamont. He looks on from the shadows with hunger in his eyes.
GRAYSON
Done!
Out of breath, Grayson leans against the wall. Above him, in dripping purple paint, a heart, a plus, and a peace sign. He spins around, raises his hands, and shouts.
GRAYSON
Love and peace!
Harley squints.
GRAYSON
I saw it in a tv show. It’s a little too idealistic for me but I appreciate the sentiment.
Harley takes a second look at the art, and smiles.
GRAYSON
Don’t get too comfy, you’re up.
Grayson crosses to the other side and slides down the wall. His light disperses the shadows at Harleys feet.
GRAYSON
You can put whatever you want, symbols, drawings, words, the words you wish you could say to them.
Harley looks deep into his can frowning, then softens. He steps up to the wall, takes a deep breath, and begins. The words form on the wall, Grayson watches in solemn silence. Delamont snakes towards Grayson from the shadows. He morphs himself a face, and stares an inch away from Grayson. A sniffle interrupts his attempt to scare him, he reforms himself on the other side to find a tear falling down Grayson’s face, then turns to Harleys work.
“I miss you more”
Harley drops the can and spins to Grayson, smiling.
HARLEY
It’s just something we always said-
Grayson wraps Harley in a hug and holds tight. He wipes the tear off his cheek, still fixed on the writing. Harley’s eyes widen, then he closes them, and leans into Grayson’s chest.
GRAYSON
It’s beautiful.
Grayson swallows hard, fake coughs, and pulls away.
GRAYSON
We should probably get going now though, or have you forgotten? The reckoning is upon us.
Harley smiles and nods, Grayson wraps his arm around him and the two make their way back down the tunnel. Delamont stands frozen in the darkness, staring at the words. He tries to laugh it off and leave, but something catches his gaze. Farther down the tunnel, a sentence painted, dripping purple.
“I deserved better”.
